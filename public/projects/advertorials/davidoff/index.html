Get involved

Even the smallest changes can make a big difference.
Here are some easy tips on what you and everyone can do to help protect our ocean.

Help clean your local area

Beach and river clean-ups are a hands-on way to prevent plastic pollution before it reaches the ocean. You can now participate in a DAVIDOFF Cool Water beach clean up in your local area organized in collaboration with National Geographic and a local organisation. You can also join a community team who hosts regular beach clean ups or organize your own.

  • Join DAVIDOFF Cool Water beach clean up
  • Join a community team
The Plastic Challenge

Participate in 'The Plastic Challenge’, attempting to avoid single-use plastic for as long as you can. Go plastic-free for a day, a week, a month or longer. You can even host a ‘Plastic Challenge Event’!

Sign a petition

There are dozens of petitions circling the internet targeting ocean pollutants, such as the proposed ban of plastic microbeads in the UK and plastic bags throughout the EU. Add your voice to their cause to help it get heard by elected officials.

Write to your local MP

There has been a 43% rise in the number of plastic bottles found on UK beaches since 2015. Deposit Return Systems have already successfully reduced the amount of littered drink containers in Australia, Germany and the US by introducing a deposit on single-use bottles and creating a financial incentive for reluctant recyclers. Write to your local MP showing your support for Deposit Return Systems.

Spread the word

Encourage friends and family to properly dispose of their plastic waste, and teach them creative ways to upcycle their plastic.

THE PRISTINE SEAS'S MISSION

Since 2012, DAVIDOFF Cool Water has supported the National Geographic Society’s Pristine Seas expeditions with the Love The Ocean initiative. The mission of Pristine Seas is to find, survey and protect marine environments across the globe, with the goal of helping to protect 10% of the world’s oceans by 2020.

In the last 5 years DAVIDOFF Cool Water has supported 14 Pristine Seas expeditions, creating 5 marine protected areas totaling 1,713,518km2.
